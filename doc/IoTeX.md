What is Crypto's Got Talent (CGT)
Image

Crypto’s Got Talent (CGT) is the world’s first reality-show-style competition for builders at the edge of Web3 and Real World AI. Instead of closed-door grants or boring pitch days, CGT puts projects on stage in front of judges, investors, and the community — live and unfiltered. Over the course of three phases — from quickfire pitches, to demo presentations, to full-blown live showcases — teams battle their way through for funding, exposure, and the chance to prove they can actually deliver. It’s part competition, part accelerator, part spectacle, designed to surface the most promising projects where AI meets the physical world, and to give them the visibility and support they need to scale.

Wavehack Timeline (CGT Phase 1, 2, & 3)
The CGT journey unfolds in three phases. Phase 1 is the pitch stage, hosted through X Spaces over roughly five episodes, running until the end of October. Here, projects introduce themselves in short pitches, and from this pool we select about 20 teams to advance. Phase 2 is the demo stage, running through November, where these 20 projects present demos, decks, or idea-stage concepts in three to four livestreamed semifinal episodes with our hosts and judges. From there, we narrow the field to the top 12. Finally, in Phase 3, those 12 compete in the finals, taking place through mid-December, where 8–10 standout projects are chosen as winners and awarded milestone-based grants.

📜 Rules
Eligibility
Open to projects at any stage working in AI, RWAI (Real World AI), RWA, or Infrastructure. Teams must demonstrate a clear connection to real-world data, hardware, or systems that extend beyond purely digital applications.

Participation

Projects must remain responsive throughout the program and meet all deliverable deadlines (e.g., pitch decks, demo videos, milestone updates).
Each team is expected to actively participate in all assigned episodes (X Spaces, livestreams, and finals) and be available for Q&A sessions with judges.
Content submissions (videos, slides, banners, articles) must follow provided guidelines and be submitted at least 72 hours before scheduled release dates.
Milestones & Grants

Winners will be awarded grants on a milestone-based system. Funds are disbursed only when agreed milestones (set with IoTeX and 0G) are met.
Milestones should focus on product development, deployment, or measurable community growth.
Promotion & Visibility

By participating, projects agree to allow IoTeX and 0G to share their content (videos, demos, articles, banners) across marketing channels.
Teams should help amplify program updates through their own communities (social reposts, Discord/Telegram announcements).
Conduct

All participants must follow a code of respect and professionalism toward judges, hosts, fellow teams, and the community.
Any form of plagiarism, misrepresentation, or fraudulent activity is grounds for immediate disqualification.
Attendance & Tech Requirements

For livestreams, teams must ensure good audio and video quality and use the branded virtual background provided.
Speakers must be on time for their session and respect time limits during pitches and Q&A.
Judging Criteria
Innovation & Originality (20%)
Is the idea novel or solving a real problem in a unique way?

Technical Feasibility (20%)
Can the team realistically deliver and scale the product?

Real-World Utility (20%)
Is there early traction, demand, or a clear path to adoption?

Team Strength (20%)
Is the team capable, experienced, and aligned with the vision?

Presentation & Storytelling (20%)
Was the project clearly and compellingly communicated during the show?
